Operating contexts

We work where politics, security, reputation, and local incentives change the commercial picture. That may mean a high risk jurisdiction, a sensitive procurement chain, a founder-led company with weak internal controls, or a market where everyone seems to know something except the person signing the cheque.

The same discipline applies to smaller businesses. A music school, clinic, contractor, or niche importer can carry serious exposure if payroll, access control, staff records, devices, customer data, and informal processes are left to habit. Small does not mean simple. It usually means fewer people carrying more risk at once.

Team

Our team includes security consultants, former law enforcement, healthcare professionals, and analysts with strong OSINT practice. We do not publish biographies as a marketing device. Staffing is matched to the matter.

The common thread is practical judgment. We prefer people who have worked around real systems, real people, and real consequences, not just slide decks.

Method

We ask what has to be decided, by whom, and by when. Then we map the unknowns that matter. Some questions need a short brief. Some need a call with the right person. Some should be left alone.

Our work uses lawful open sources, structured interviews where appropriate, OSINT, data analysis, and plain judgment. The final product is usually short, because the useful part is the answer, not the theatre around it.

Data handling

We minimize intake, compartmentalize access, and keep corporate files in an encrypted server environment managed through Proton Business services in Switzerland.

We do not need every document to understand the first question. Early contact should be spare: the decision, the timeline, the jurisdiction, and who referred you. Sensitive material can wait until the right handling channel is agreed.

We respond to lawful requests for information consistent with what is required and what is permitted under applicable Swiss privacy laws. Clients should take their own legal advice on jurisdiction-specific issues.
